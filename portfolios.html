Portfolio Development

Showcase Your Work with a Professional Portfolio

A well-crafted portfolio is essential for UMSI students and graduates. It's your opportunity to demonstrate your skills, creativity, and impact through real examples of your work. Whether you're pursuing UX design, data science, information analysis, or any other SI field, a portfolio helps employers understand what you can do beyond what's listed on your resume.

Your portfolio should tell the story of your professional growth, showcase your best work, and highlight the skills most relevant to your target roles. This page will guide you through creating a portfolio that stands out to employers and effectively communicates your value as a candidate.

Why You Need a Portfolio

  • Show, Don't Just Tell: Demonstrate your actual skills and capabilities through concrete examples
  • Stand Out from Competition: Differentiate yourself from other candidates with similar qualifications
  • Tell Your Story: Provide context for your experiences and show your thought process
  • Demonstrate Growth: Show how you've developed and improved your skills over time
  • Industry Expectation: Many employers in information and technology fields expect to see portfolios

Types of Portfolios for UMSI Students

UX/UI Design Portfolio

  • User research studies and findings
  • Wireframes and prototypes
  • Design process documentation
  • Before and after design iterations
  • Usability testing results

Data Science Portfolio

  • Data analysis projects with clear methodologies
  • Data visualizations and dashboards
  • Machine learning models and results
  • Code samples and GitHub repositories
  • Statistical analysis and insights

Information Analysis Portfolio

  • Research projects and methodologies
  • Database design and implementation
  • Systems analysis documentation
  • Information architecture examples
  • Process improvement initiatives

General Information Portfolio

  • Academic and personal projects
  • Problem-solving examples
  • Technical writing samples
  • Collaborative project contributions
  • Leadership and initiative examples

Portfolio Building Steps

  1. Audit Your Work: Collect all projects, assignments, and experiences that demonstrate your skills
  2. Choose Your Best Work: Select 3-5 high-quality pieces that showcase different skills and approaches
  3. Document Your Process: For each piece, explain your approach, challenges, and solutions
  4. Choose a Platform: Select a website builder or create a custom site to host your portfolio
  5. Design for User Experience: Make your portfolio easy to navigate and visually appealing
  6. Write Compelling Descriptions: Tell the story behind each project with clear, engaging explanations
  7. Get Feedback: Ask peers, professors, and career coaches to review your portfolio
  8. Keep It Updated: Regularly add new work and remove outdated pieces

Portfolio Platforms and Tools

Website Builders

  • Squarespace: Professional templates, easy to use, good for visual portfolios
  • Wix: Drag-and-drop interface, many customization options
  • WordPress: Flexible platform with many themes and plugins
  • Adobe Portfolio: Integrated with Creative Cloud, great for design portfolios

Specialized Platforms

  • GitHub Pages: Free hosting for technical portfolios and documentation
  • Behance: Creative portfolio platform, good for design work
  • Dribbble: Design-focused platform for showcasing visual work
  • Kaggle: Great for data science projects and competitions

Custom Solutions

  • HTML/CSS/JavaScript: Build a completely custom portfolio website
  • React/Vue/Angular: Create an interactive, dynamic portfolio
  • PDF Portfolio: Create a downloadable document for offline viewing

Portfolio Best Practices

Content Guidelines

  • Lead with your strongest work
  • Include a variety of project types and skills
  • Explain your role in group projects
  • Show the impact and results of your work
  • Include both academic and professional work when relevant

Design Principles

  • Keep the design clean and professional
  • Ensure easy navigation between projects
  • Use high-quality images and screenshots
  • Make sure your portfolio is mobile-responsive
  • Include clear contact information

Technical Considerations

  • Optimize images for fast loading times
  • Test your portfolio on different devices and browsers
  • Ensure all links work properly
  • Consider SEO if you want your portfolio discoverable
  • Keep your portfolio URL simple and professional

Getting Portfolio Feedback

A great portfolio is rarely created in isolation. Getting feedback from multiple perspectives will help you create a stronger, more effective showcase of your work.

Who to Ask for Feedback

  • Career Coaches: Get professional advice on portfolio strategy and presentation
  • Faculty: Receive academic perspective on your work and technical accuracy
  • Industry Professionals: Gain insights from practitioners in your target field
  • Peers: Get honest feedback from classmates and fellow students
  • Alumni: Learn from graduates who have successfully entered your target industry
