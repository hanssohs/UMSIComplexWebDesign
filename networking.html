Networking & Building Connections

Build Your Professional Network

Networking isn't just about collecting business cards or LinkedIn connections—it's about building meaningful professional relationships that can support your career growth. Whether you're an introvert or extrovert, there are networking strategies that work for every personality type.

At UMSI, you have access to an incredible network of alumni, faculty, and industry professionals who are eager to help current students succeed. Learn how to tap into this network and build lasting professional relationships.

Why Networking Matters

  • Hidden Job Market: Many positions are never publicly posted—networking helps you discover these opportunities.
  • Industry Insights: Connect with professionals to learn about trends, company cultures, and career paths.
  • Mentorship Opportunities: Build relationships with experienced professionals who can guide your career development.
  • Referrals and Recommendations: Strong connections can lead to job referrals and professional recommendations.
  • Professional Growth: Expand your knowledge and skills through conversations with industry experts.

Getting Started with Networking

  1. Start with Your Existing Network: Family, friends, classmates, professors, and former colleagues can provide valuable connections.
  2. Optimize Your LinkedIn Profile: Create a professional profile that showcases your skills and interests.
  3. Attend UMSI Events: Participate in career fairs, alumni events, and industry speaker series.
  4. Join Professional Organizations: Become a member of organizations related to your field of interest.
  5. Conduct Informational Interviews: Reach out to professionals to learn about their career paths and industries.
  6. Follow Up and Stay Connected: Maintain relationships through regular communication and updates.

Networking Tips for Success

Before the Event

  • Research attendees and companies that will be present
  • Prepare your elevator pitch (30-60 second introduction)
  • Set specific goals for the number of connections you want to make
  • Bring business cards or have your contact information ready

During the Event

  • Ask open-ended questions about their work and experiences
  • Listen actively and show genuine interest
  • Share your own experiences and goals authentically
  • Exchange contact information when appropriate

After the Event

  • Send personalized follow-up messages within 24-48 hours
  • Reference specific topics you discussed
  • Connect on LinkedIn with a personalized message
  • Follow through on any commitments you made

Virtual Networking

Online networking has become increasingly important. Here are ways to build connections virtually:

  • LinkedIn Engagement: Comment thoughtfully on posts and share relevant content
  • Virtual Events: Attend online industry events and webinars
  • Professional Communities: Join relevant online groups and forums
  • Video Coffee Chats: Schedule informal virtual meetings with connections
  • Social Media Presence: Share your professional insights and achievements
